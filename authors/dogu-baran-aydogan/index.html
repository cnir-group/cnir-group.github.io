Dogu Baran Aydogan

Group leader, Academy Research Fellow

A.I. Virtanen Institute for Molecular Sciences, University of Eastern Finland

Department of Neuroscience and Biomedical Engineering, Aalto University School of Science

Dogu Baran Aydogan, PhD, leads the CNIR group. He is an Academy of Finland Research Fellow at A.I. Virtanen Institute for Molecular Sciences, University of Eastern Finland in Kuopio, Finland. He is at the same time a Visiting Researcher at the Department of Neuroscience and Biomedical Engineering, Aalto University in Espoo, Finland.

His research focuses on developing methods for medical image processing and connectivity analysis, particularly in the field of diffusion MRI-based tractography. His doctoral research laid the foundation for his postdoctoral work at the Laboratory of Neuro Imaging (LONI) at the University of Southern California, where he and Professor Yonggang Shi studied the structural connectivity of the brain and developed new techniques to analyze white matter connections.

Dr. Aydogan has received numerous awards for his works, including the MICCAI Young Scientist Award in 2016 for his probabilistic tractography framework and victories in the TraCED (ISMRM 2017), VoTEM (ISBI 2018), IronTract (MICCAI 2019-2020), and Disco (MICCAI 2021) international tractography challenges. He is also a co-inventor of a real-time image analysis method for medical applications using mobile devices (US patent: 11112406), and has developed Trekker, an open-source implementation of his tractography algorithms.

In addition to his research, Dr. Aydogan has experience with real-time image processing and has implemented a real-time tractography-based transcranial magnetic stimulation (TMS) neuronavigation system, which won the best abstract award at the 11th International Symposium on nTMS, Berlin, 2019, and the best poster award at the 4th Brain Stimulation Conference, Charleston, 2021. His latest study presents a novel tractography technique that takes advantage of the topographic organization of connections in the brain.
